# Who's Hatsune Miku
## (English Pratice)

Hatsune Miku is a software project from Crypton Future Media aimed to create a
virtual singer. Her first release was in August 31th, 2017. Since that, anyone
became able to buy a license and record a song with Hatsune Miku in the vocals.
The first version only supported Japanese lyrics, but recent versions (the
latest stable release was in 2020) also support English and Chinese.

The software was developed using Yamaha's Vocaloid synthetizer technology, and
the voice was modeled from Japanese voice actress Saki Fujita. To record a song
with it, music producers compose lyrics and chords just like a normal song with
a real singer, and the computer generate every single language phonic that are
strung together to create entire phrases that Miku "sings".

As anyone can buy a license and record music with her, Miku is probably the
singer with more recorded songs in the world. Probably her most famous recording
is a shot of the traditional Finish song Ievan Polkka, uploaded to YouTube by an
unknown producer.

Hatsune Miku is an interesting artist because she does not exist in the real,
material world. Miku is just a software project and a character that people use
to create their music. Miku does not live in our world, but in our songs.
